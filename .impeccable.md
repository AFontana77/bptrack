# BPTrack — Impeccable Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
People managing high blood pressure, cardiac patients, caregivers tracking a parent's readings. They're at home in the morning or evening, taking a reading, logging it. Sometimes anxious. Always wanting clear numbers, normal-range guidance, and a sense of control. AHA-grade clinical context but consumer-friendly delivery.

### Brand Personality
Three words: **clinical, calm, reassuring.**

Healthcare-grade without feeling cold. The site should feel like a well-designed medical reference, not a hospital intake form. Clear numbers, clear guidance, zero panic.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Light theme.** Medical context, anxiety-friendly bright background, daytime use.

**Font pair:**
- Display: `Domine` — slab-ish serif with medical/encyclopedic feel. Reads like a medical reference book or a USPSTF guideline document. NOT Newsreader or Lora (reflex serifs).
- Body: `Work Sans` — clean, neutral, slightly geometric humanist sans. Excellent legibility for numerical data.

**Color system (OKLCH, light, warm-tinted):**
- Background: `oklch(0.99 0.003 20)` — near-white with very slight warm pink tint
- Surface: `oklch(0.96 0.008 20)` — warm white
- Foreground: `oklch(0.18 0.02 20)` — near-black with warm tint
- Muted foreground: `oklch(0.48 0.015 20)`
- Border: `oklch(0.86 0.012 20)`
- Primary (medical burgundy): `oklch(0.45 0.18 25)` — serious red, not panic red
- Primary dark: `oklch(0.36 0.16 25)`
- Accent (calm cyan): `oklch(0.62 0.12 200)` — for normal-range indicators

### Design Principles
1. **Numbers are the focal point.** Blood pressure readings are the value — display them with weight.
2. **Calm authority.** No urgent red badges, no scary alarms in the marketing pages.
3. **Left-aligned editorial.** Like a reference book, not a centered brochure.
4. **No icon card grids.** Replace with table-like data presentations.
5. **AHA-quoted facts get visual weight** — they're the credibility anchor.
